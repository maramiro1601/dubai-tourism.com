About the Burj Khalifa

The Burj Khalifa, located in Dubai, United Arab Emirates, is the tallest building in the world, standing at 828 meters (2,717 feet).

It has more than 160 stories, the most of any building in the world, and includes residential, office, and retail spaces, as well as a hotel designed by Giorgio Armani.

Construction of the Burj Khalifa began in 2004, with the exterior completed five years later in 2009.

The real magic happens when you ascend its heights and view the world from its observation decks.

Standing as the world's tallest building, the Burj Khalifa is just an architectural marvel; it's a symbol of modern human ambition and engineering prowess.

The observation deck on the 124th floor offers an unparalleled view of Dubai.

Its construction used 330,000 cubic meters of concrete and 39,000 tonnes of steel rebar.
